## Overview

This repository houses 20 fundamental scientific laws that guide responsible, effective, and reproducible AI development. Each law is documented with rigorous definitions, mathematical foundations, and practical applications.

## The 20 Scientific Laws for AI Agents

1. Law of Reproducibility - All AI agent behaviors must be reproducible under identical conditions
2. Law of Verifiable Outcomes - All outcomes must be independently verifiable
3. Law of Controlled Variables - Variables must be systematically controlled in experiments
4. Law of Statistical Significance - Results must demonstrate statistical significance
5. Law of Computational Efficiency - Solutions must optimize computational resources
6. Law of Data Integrity - Data must maintain complete integrity throughout processing
7. Law of Algorithmic Transparency - Algorithms must be interpretable and transparent
8. Law of Error Bounds - All errors must have defined and measurable bounds
9. Law of Convergence - Systems must demonstrate convergence properties
10. Law of Scalability - Solutions must scale effectively with problem size
11. Law of Resource Conservation - Resources must be conserved and optimized
12. Law of Information Preservation - Information must be preserved through transformations
13. Law of Causal Inference - Causal relationships must be rigorously established
14. Law of Model Uncertainty - Uncertainty must be quantified and bounded
15. Law of Ethical Constraints - All operations must respect ethical boundaries
16. Law of Adversarial Robustness - Systems must resist adversarial perturbations
17. Law of Generalization Limits - Generalization boundaries must be defined
18. Law of Temporal Consistency - Behavior must remain consistent across time
19. Law of Knowledge Integration - Knowledge must be properly integrated from sources
20. Law of Continuous Validation - Systems must undergo continuous validation
